Kit vs Custom

Kit Vs. Custom

Over the last few years, FTC robotics has seem a rapid transition from manufacturer-created "kit" parts to custom solutions to game challenges. This begs the question, "Which is better?". While there is no simple answer to this, I wil attempt to give my thoughts on the matter below.

Especially in the North Carolina region, it seems as though custom is on the rise. Many teams are attempting advanced custom solutions to problems. However, I believe that there is a fundamental flaw in this approach. There is no need for custom parts if you are not already getting the full strength out of manufacturer-produced parts . Similar to my views on odometry, I believe that teams should master the use of standard products before moving on to custom.

Even then, I am not convinced of the neccesity of custom. If you take a look at the past season, Skystone, you will see that none of the top 5 teams in the world were full custom robots This is evidence enough to me that, while custom parts are cool and have their uses, a hybrid approach to robot design that involves both manufacturer-produced parts and custom parts when needed is currently optimal.
